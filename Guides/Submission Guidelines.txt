AI Hackathon Guidelines: A Step-by-Step Tutorial

Participating in a hackathon can be an incredibly rewarding experience, offering opportunities to learn, collaborate, and innovate. This tutorial outlines the essential steps to follow, from initial registration to final project submission, ensuring you have a smooth and successful hackathon journey.

AI hackathons are a focused type of artificial intelligence hackathon where you build practical AI projects in a short, intensive timeframe. Whether you're joining online AI hackathons or competing in global AI hackathons on LabLab.ai, these guidelines will help you move smoothly from signup to final submission. To discover upcoming AI hackathons and pick your next event, explore LabLab.ai's global AI hackathons.

Watch the AI Hackathon Guidelines Video

If you prefer a visual walkthrough, you can also follow these steps in a short video overview:


1. Understanding the AI Hackathon Process

To effectively participate, it's crucial to understand the typical flow of an AI hackathon. Generally, it involves several key stages designed to guide you through the process of developing and presenting your artificial intelligence project under time constraints.

Steps to Follow:

Complete your lablab.ai profile.
Register for the hackathon.
Connect your Discord account.
Create or join a team.
Submit your project.
2. Signing Up for the AI Hackathon

The first official step is to enroll in your chosen AI hackathon. Navigate to the hackathon's specific URL on LabLab.ai, the AI hackathon platform where most events are hosted online.

Click on the prominent green "Enrol Now" button, usually located at the bottom of the screen or on the main event page.

Enrol Now button on a hackathon page
The 'Enrol Now' button to begin your hackathon registration.
If you haven't already, you will be prompted to sign up for a lablab.ai account and create your participant profile before proceeding.

3. Forming Your AI Hackathon Team

Collaboration is a cornerstone of AI hackathons. The next step involves joining an existing team or creating your own AI hackathon team. This is a critical phase for brainstorming AI hackathon project ideas and distributing tasks.

Interface for creating or joining a hackathon team
Options to create a new team or join an existing one.
How to Create a Team:

First, ensure your Discord account is connected to your lablab.ai profile. If your profile is incomplete, you'll be prompted to finish it.

Prompt to connect Discord account
Connect your Discord for seamless team collaboration and communication.
Once connected, return to the hackathon page and click the "Create or Join a team" button. Select "Create a team" and fill in the required details for your new team.

Button to create a new team
Click here to establish your hackathon team.
Form to fill in team details
Provide essential information for your team's profile.
You can then invite teammates to join your newly formed team or allow others to request to join.

How to Find/Join a Team:

If you're looking for teammates, there are two primary methods:

Browse Existing Teams: Scroll down the hackathon page and look for teams displaying the "looking for members" badge. Navigate to their dashboard and use the chat function to see if there's a good match for your skills and interests.
Discord Channel: Join the dedicated Discord server for the hackathon and check the #looking-for-team channel. Post a message introducing yourself and your skills, or respond to existing requests.
4. Submitting Your AI Hackathon Project

After all your hard work in developing your solution, the final step is to submit your AI hackathon project. This involves providing detailed information about your application so judges and the AI hackathon community can clearly understand your work.

Scroll down the hackathon page and click on the "Submit project" button.

Submit project button on hackathon page
Initiate the project submission process.
You will then be guided through a form to fill in the following details:

Project Information:

Submission Title: A concise title for your project (max 50 characters).
Short Description (Summary): A brief overview of your project (max 255 characters).
Long Description: A comprehensive explanation of your AI hackathon idea (minimum 100 words).
Main Tracks: Select the primary categories your project aligns with (these are usually listed on the hackathon page).
Technologies: List all technologies used in your project (a comprehensive list is often available at lablab.ai/tech). This helps judges understand how you applied AI technology within the hackathon.
Click "Next Step" to proceed.

Media Uploads:

Cover Image: Upload a visually appealing cover image for your project (recommended 16:9 ratio).
Video Presentation: Provide a link to your video presentation (ensure it's under 300MB and within 5 minutes duration).
Click "Next Step" to proceed.

Technical Details:

GitHub Repository: Link to your project's GitHub repository (e.g., https://github.com/your-username/your-project). If you have multiple repositories, mention them within your README.md file.
Demo Application Platform: Specify the platform where your application is deployed (for example, a cloud platform commonly used for AI hackathon projects).
Demo Application URL: Provide the direct URL to your live application demo.
Additional Information: Include any other relevant details or considerations for the judges, such as how your solution could scale beyond the AI hackathon.
Finally, click the "Submit" button to finalize your project submission. You're now good to go and ready to showcase your AI hackathon project to the judges and community!

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an AI hackathon?

An AI hackathon is a time-limited artificial intelligence hackathon where participants build and ship AI projects, such as chatbots, agents, or generative AI apps. Teams typically have a few days to move from idea to working demo and then present their projects to judges.

How can I use these guidelines in an AI hackathon on LabLab.ai?

You can follow this tutorial as a checklist for any AI hackathon hosted on LabLab.ai—from creating your profile and connecting Discord to forming a team and submitting your project. Treat each section as a step in your AI hackathon journey so you never miss a requirement or deadline.

Are AI hackathons on LabLab.ai suitable for beginners?

Yes, many AI hackathons on LabLab.ai welcome beginners and provide learning resources, mentor support, and clear submission guidelines. If you're new, look for AI hackathons for beginners and join teams that are open to helping newcomers learn.

Are AI hackathons free, and how do I join one?

Most online AI hackathons on LabLab.ai are free to join—simply enroll on the event page, connect your profile, and create or join a team. You can browse upcoming AI hackathons on LabLab.ai and pick the event that best matches your interests and skill level.

What are some good AI hackathon project ideas?

Strong AI hackathon project ideas often solve real problems with AI agents, chatbots, recommendation systems, or generative AI applications. Think about use cases in areas like education, healthcare, finance, or productivity that you can prototype quickly during the hackathon.

Conclusion

Following these guidelines will help ensure a structured and successful participation in any AI hackathon on LabLab.ai. Remember to leverage the community, utilize available resources, and focus on clear communication in your AI hackathon project and presentation. Good luck, and happy hacking!
